Who We Are

Seclusive Utah was created with the ideal of improving the recreational experiences of all nature lovers by directing users of our website to the many lesser known parks and recreational areas within the state of Utah.
Are you looking for a good place to see the amazing array of stars lost to view because of light pollution in major cities? Seclusive Utah has a list of park areas that have been internationally recognized for their 'dark sky' spectacular views of the stars; unpolluted by human generated light sources.
Perhaps you instead want a camping site where you can truly escape the crowds and bustle of city life? Well, Seclusive Utah has a list of the least visited parks in Utah; based upon the Park Services 2025 visitation report. Recreation areas on that list that also include the "dark sky" criteria can truly offer a unique and seclusive experience for site users. Should your experience prove different for a specific recreation site that we list, simply contact us using our "contact page" link listed above; and we will remove that site from the list, and note it in our monthly Newsletter.

Our Commitment

As part of our effort to improve recreational experience, we at Seclusive Utah are committed to maintaining Utah's recreation areas as pristine and natural as possible. We hold the paradigm, and we strongly encourage our site users to follow it's practice to "leave it better than you found it." This means we remove our own waste from recreation areas; as well as any that may have been left by other, less responsible recreators.
That is not to say that we expect a few lone individuals to manage the entire endeavor of keeping Utah Pristine. To meet our goals to maintain recreation areas as clean and natural as possible we partner with local park rangers and others who share our values for monthly gatherings at selected sites to clean and restore them back to their natural state and beauty. If the effort to keep 'civilation', i.e. waste from ruining the natural beauty of recreation areas for your loved ones and future generations please submit to our Newsletter using the link below; to receive news and updates on our site cleanup initiative and gathering dates; as well as updated information on our list of recreational sites.
